Basic Information

Click on the tiles below to reveal more information

Capital

Douglas

Douglas has been the capital of the Isle of Man since 1869, prior to this it was Castletown ("The Ancient Capital of Mann").

Due to increasing transport links with Liverpool during the 19th century and expanding population in Douglas, it was decided to change the capital to Douglas.

Population

83,314 people

According the 2016 government census, 32% of people reside in the capital, Douglas.

The population is mainly British (92%) with the remainder being European (5%), Asian (1%), African (1%) and other (1%).

Click here for more population statistics

Language

English and Manx Gaelic

The main language in the Isle of Man is English, but the Manx language remains very much part of the culture. Most road signs include the Manx translation and announcements on the ferry are given in Manx as well as English.

Flag

Three legs of Mann

The three legs on the flag represent the latin motto "Quocunque Jeceris Stabit" which translates to "Whithersoever you throw it, it will stand".

Currency

Manx Pound

Although there is no exchange rate difference with the British pound, it is commonly used around the island. The British pound can be used on the island.

Nationality

Manx

The Isle of Man is an independent island nation so people born here are Manx.

It is the smallest of five countries within Great Britain (England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland and the Isle of Man).

Climate

Temperate

The island's climate is temperate but lacks extremes. For example, it rarely snows but when it does, it normally melts pretty quickly.

In contrast the summers tend to seen an average maximum temperature of 18 degrees celsius

Government

Tynwald

The Isle of Man is not part of UK and is self governed by Tynwald, the Manx government. The IoM sets its own laws independent of UK parliament with the UK having no authority to change laws on the island.

Did you know...

Isle of Woman

Did you know that the Isle of Man was the first place in Britain to give women the right to vote? After Lydia Becker (leader of the suffragette movement in Manchester) visited the island, she rallied and inspired the women of the Isle of Man to stand up and fight for their right to vote. This let to the Tynwald debate and the eventual change in legislation granting women the right to vote.

Purrrr-fect

Did you know that the Isle of Man has it's own native breed of cat? They are very different to other breeds. One unusual characteristic is when they run, they tend to hop like a rabbit rather run like other breeds of cat.

The Manx cat is a rare breed of cat and they lack a key body part. Which body part is missing?

Tail
